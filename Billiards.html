Billiards

Billiards is a table game played with a stick (cue) and balls, focusing on skill and accuracy.

Introduction of Billiards

Billiards is a traditional indoor cue sport played on a rectangular table with balls and a cue stick. The objective is to score points by striking the cue ball to hit other balls in a specific way. It requires focus, precision, and strategy, and is popular worldwide as both a casual and competitive game.

How Many Players Play the Game?

Billiards is usually played by 2 players, who take turns to score points by hitting balls with a cue stick. In some formats, it can also be played in teams of two (doubles).

Is it a team game?

Yes, Billiards can be a team game when played in the doubles format, where two players team up against another pair. However, it is most commonly played as a one-on-one game.

Rule of the game

Objective

The goal is to score more points than your opponent by making legal shots with three balls on the table:

  • White cue ball (for Player A)
  • Yellow or spot white cue ball (for Player B)
  • Red object ball

Scoring Methods

In billiards, players can score in three main ways. First is potting, where you strike your cue ball to pocket another ball ‚Äî potting the red ball earns 3 points, and potting the opponent's cue ball gives 2 points. The second method is a cannon (carom), where your cue ball hits both the red and the opponent's cue ball in a single shot, scoring 2 points.

Turn Rules

  • A player continues playing until they miss a scoring shot or commit a foul.
  • Players alternate turns when no legal shot is made.

Common Fouls

  • Hitting the wrong ball first
  • Cue ball jumps off the table
  • Making no contact with any ball
  • Potting the cue ball without a valid hit

Winning

  • The game can be played to a set score (e.g., 100 or 200 points) or for a fixed time, where the highest score at the end wins

Important Facts About Billiards

Origin

Billiards originated in France in the 15th century as an outdoor lawn game, later adapted to indoor tables.

Types of Billiards

There are different versions including English Billiards, Carom Billiards (no pockets), and Pool (like 8-ball or 9-ball).

Table Design

A standard billiards table has no pockets in carom games, while English Billiards and Pool tables have six pockets.

Three Balls Only

In English Billiards, only three balls are used ‚Äî red, white, and yellow or spot white.

Cue Stick

All shots are made using a cue stick, and precision, angles, and spin (called English) are key skills.

Scoring

Points are earned through potting, cannons, and in-offs. Strategy and control are more important than power.

Statistics / Records

Billiards has a rich history of remarkable performances and legendary players. One of the most outstanding records is held by Walter Lindrum of Australia, who made an incredible break of 4,137 points in a single turn during a match in 1932 ‚Äî a world record that still stands today. He dominated the game in his era and is widely regarded as one of the greatest billiards players in history.

In recent decades, players like Mike Russell from England and Pankaj Advani from India have set new standards in modern billiards. Pankaj Advani is particularly notable for being the only player to win world titles in both English Billiards and Snooker, earning multiple international championships and setting new milestones in the sport.

These records highlight the precision, skill, and mental focus required to excel in billiards at the highest level.
