Introduction of Football

Football is a globally celebrated sport of strength, strategy, and spirit, where two teams of eleven players battle on the field, aiming to conquer the game through coordination, precision, and sheer passion.

How Many Players Play the Game?

Football is played between two teams of eleven players each, including one goalkeeper and ten outfield players‚Äîdefenders, midfielders, and forwards.

Is it a team game?

Yes, football is a team game. It is played between two teams, each consisting of 11 players, and requires teamwork and coordination to succeed.

Rule of the game

  • Duration: A football match is 90 minutes long, divided into two halves of 45 minutes with a 15-minute halftime break.
  • Offside Rule: A player is offside if they are nearer to the opponent‚Äôs goal line than both the ball and the second-last defender when the ball is played to them.
  • Fouls: Pushing, tripping, holding, or deliberate handball by a player is considered a foul.
  • Penalties: Fouls committed inside the penalty area result in a penalty kick for the opposing team.
  • Goalkeeper Rules: The goalkeeper can only use hands within their penalty area and cannot pick up a back-pass from a teammate.
  • Substitutions: Up to 5 substitutions are allowed in most matches for player rotation or injury.
  • Cards: A yellow card is a warning; two yellows or a straight red card leads to the player being sent off.
  • Kick-Off: The match starts with a kick-off from the center circle and resumes in the same way after each goal.

Important Facts About Football

Invented

Modern football, as we know it, was codified in 1863 when the Football Association (FA) was formed in England.

FIFA World Cup

The FIFA World Cup is the most prestigious tournament in football. It is held every four years and is contested by national teams from around the world. The first World Cup was held in 1930 in Uruguay.

Other Tournaments
